The First intellectual & Spiritual retreat in Cambridge

CAMBRIDGE, UK. 6-9 JUNE 2024

About the Event

An intellectual and spiritual retreat in the mythical landscape of Cambridge and its historical university. This trip is exceptionally designed to explore the history of science and religion at the heart of Cambridge life. Amid existential risks such as pollution and global warming, the nuclear threat, pandemics and artificial intelligence, the aim of this retreat is to understand our historical role as Muslim subjects and to discuss our fate as human beings with a Cambridge scholar who is embedded in both Islamic tradition and modern academia. Home of 31 colleges and 121 affiliated Nobel Prize winners, along with the Cambridge Central Mosque as the first eco-mosque in Europe, Cambridge is the ideal place to engage our intellect while refreshing our spirit. Along the way, we will visit the famous Kings College and its chapel, as well as Darwin College, the first graduate-only college, to explore its splendid gardens on the riverside. We will punt in the River Cam, walk along the backs of the colleges and stroll through the inner circle of the city to appreciate its Gothic style of architecture. We will discover the history behind the Trinity gate, the Newton Tree, and the Mathematical Bridge and enjoy food in the multicultural traditional markets and roads. We will have a guided tour at the Cambridge Central Mosque, and experience gatherings of dhikr accompanied by experienced munshids.

SCHEDULE

The following itinerary describes a range of colleges and landmarks we plan to visit. Many are accessible to the public, but others require special permission which may only be confirmed closer to the retreat’s departure in June 2024. The daily activities described in this itinerary may change or be rotated and/or modified in order to accommodate alterations in opening hours, transport schedules and confirmation of private visits. Participants will receive a final itinerary together with their retreat documents and readings prior to departure. Meals included in the tour price and are indicated in the itinerary that will be shared with participants where: B=breakfast, L=light lunch or picnic lunch and D=dinner.

SEPTEMBER 15, 2021

Wednesday 5 June

Registration, Check In, and the College Porters’ indications regarding safety and well being.

Thursday 6 June

We start our program with the opening lecture: Introduction to the Modern World. In this lecture, we explore the intellectual genealogy of the modern world. We will answer the questions ‘how’ and ‘why’ we are currently living the life that we’re living. We will also enjoy the university ’s riverside gardens as a reflection of the pre-modern condition. We will take our lunch, discover the University library and the surrounding faculties before gathering for dhikr. .

Friday 7 June

We will start the day with the second lecture on the core topic of this retreat: Science and Religion. The lecture will be followed by a guided tour at one of the most beautiful buildings in the world: the Cambridge Central Mosque. After the Jumu’a prayer, we will enjoy lunch at an authentic restaurant before heading towards the inner city to discover the university colleges and end our day with a dhikr gathering led by one of the Cambridge-based scholars.

Saturday 8 June

We will start the day with a deep reflection on the nature of modern education in comparison with the pre-modern settings of teaching and learning. This lecture will be delivered by a guest lecturer. It will be followed by lunch and a punting tour to discover the stunning landscape of the university colleges from the wonderful River Cam. Participants will have free time afterwards to enjoy the city by their own.
